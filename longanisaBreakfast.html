Longaniza Breakfast Plate

20 mins prep 2 servings Easy

Start your day the Filipino way with our flavorful JCL Longaniza served with garlic fried rice, sunny-side-up eggs, and tangy atchara. This classic breakfast combination delivers a perfect balance of sweet, savory, and tangy flavors that will energize your morning.

Ingredients

  • 4 links JCL Longaniza
  • 2 cups Cooked white rice (preferably day-old)
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Cooking oil
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1/2 cup Atchara (pickled green papaya)
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 1 Tomato, sliced (optional)
  • 1 Cucumber, sliced (optional)
  • To taste Salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the longaniza: Prick each sausage link several times with a fork to prevent bursting. In a pan over medium heat, add 1/2 cup water and the longaniza. Cover and let simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Uncover and continue cooking, turning occasionally, until water evaporates and longaniza is browned and cooked through (about 8-10 minutes total). Remove from pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add 1 tbsp oil and minced garlic. Cook over medium heat until golden brown and fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  5. Add soy sauce and black pepper to the rice, mixing well to distribute evenly. Cook for another 2 minutes until rice is heated through. Remove from pan and set aside.
  6. In the same pan, add remaining oil and fry eggs sunny-side-up or to your preferred doneness. Season with salt.
  7. To serve: Divide garlic rice between two plates. Arrange 2 longaniza links on each plate beside the rice. Place a fried egg on top of the rice.
  8. Garnish with atchara and optional tomato and cucumber slices on the side.
  9. Serve immediately with banana ketchup or spicy vinegar if desired.

Chef's Notes

Variations: Add diced onions to the garlic rice for extra flavor. For a complete Filipino breakfast, include a small bowl of champorado (chocolate rice porridge).

Storage: Cooked longaniza can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Reheat gently in a pan with a little water.

Serving Suggestion: Pair with hot chocolate or coffee for an authentic Filipino breakfast experience.
