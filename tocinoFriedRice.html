Tocino Fried Rice

25 mins prep 4 servings Easy

Transform leftover rice into a flavorful meal with our sweet and savory JCL Tocino. This Filipino-style fried rice combines caramelized tocino pieces with garlic-infused rice and scrambled eggs for a satisfying breakfast or quick dinner that's packed with flavor.

Ingredients

  • 400g JCL Tocino, sliced into small pieces
  • 4 cups Day-old cooked rice
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp Cooking oil
  • 3 Eggs, beaten
  • 1 Small onion, diced
  • 2 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Fish sauce (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup Green onions, chopped
  • 1 Tomato, sliced (for garnish)
  • 1 Cucumber, sliced (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the tocino: In a pan over medium heat, add 1/2 cup water and the sliced tocino. Simmer uncovered for 8-10 minutes until water evaporates and tocino caramelizes. Remove tocino from pan, reserving 1 tbsp of the rendered fat.
  2. In the same pan, add 1 tbsp oil to the reserved tocino fat. Saut√© garlic and onions until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Push garlic and onions to one side of the pan. Pour beaten eggs on the other side and scramble until just set.
  4. Add the cooked rice to the pan, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes to combine with the egg mixture.
  5. Add soy sauce, fish sauce (if using), and black pepper. Mix well to distribute seasonings evenly.
  6. Return the cooked tocino pieces to the pan and stir to combine with the rice.
  7. Continue cooking for another 3-4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until everything is heated through and flavors are well blended.
  8. Turn off heat and mix in chopped green onions.
  9. Transfer to serving plates and garnish with tomato and cucumber slices.
  10. Serve hot with banana ketchup or spicy vinegar on the side.

Chef's Notes

Variations: Add diced carrots and peas for extra vegetables. For spicier version, include chopped chili peppers or drizzle with chili oil.

Storage: Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Reheat in a pan with a little oil to restore texture.

Serving Suggestion: Pair with atchara (pickled papaya) and a fried egg for a complete Filipino breakfast meal.
