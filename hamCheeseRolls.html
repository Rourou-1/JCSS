Ham and Cheese Rolls

30 mins prep 12 pieces Medium

These irresistible puff pastry rolls filled with our premium JCL Slice Ham and melted cheese make the perfect party appetizer or afternoon snack. Flaky, buttery pastry wrapped around savory ham and gooey cheese creates a crowd-pleasing treat that's surprisingly easy to make.

Ingredients

  • 1 sheet Puff pastry (about 250g), thawed
  • 150g JCL Slice Ham
  • 1 cup Shredded cheese (cheddar, mozzarella, or Swiss)
  • 1 Egg, beaten (for egg wash)
  • 1 tbsp Dijon mustard (optional)
  • 1 tsp Dried oregano or Italian seasoning
  • 1/4 tsp Garlic powder
  • To taste Black pepper
  • 1 tbsp Sesame seeds or poppy seeds (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface to about 30x20cm (12x8 inches).
  3. If using, spread a thin layer of Dijon mustard evenly over the pastry.
  4. Layer the JCL ham slices evenly over the pastry, leaving a 1cm border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle shredded cheese evenly over the ham, then season with oregano, garlic powder, and black pepper.
  6. Starting from the long side, tightly roll up the pastry into a log. Pinch the seam to seal.
  7. Using a sharp knife, cut the log into 12 equal pieces (about 2cm thick each).
  8. Place the rolls cut-side up on the prepared baking sheet, spacing them about 2cm apart.
  9. Brush the tops with beaten egg and sprinkle with sesame or poppy seeds if desired.
  10. Bake for 18-20 minutes until golden brown and puffed.
  11. Let cool for 5 minutes before serving warm.

Chef's Notes

Variations: Add sautéed onions or spinach between the ham and cheese layers. For a sweet-savory version, add a thin layer of fruit preserves.

Make Ahead: Assemble rolls up to 1 day in advance and refrigerate until ready to bake. Add 2-3 minutes to baking time if baking from cold.

Serving Suggestion: Serve with a side of honey mustard or marinara sauce for dipping. Perfect for brunch buffets or as party finger food.
