Siomai Fried Rice

15 mins prep 3 servings Easy

Transform leftover rice into a delicious and satisfying meal with this quick Siomai Fried Rice recipe. Our JCL siomai adds a burst of flavor to classic garlic fried rice, combined with scrambled eggs and vegetables for a complete one-pan dish that's perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • 12 pieces JCL Pork or Chicken Siomai
  • 3 cups Day-old cooked rice
  • 3 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp Cooking oil
  • 2 Eggs, beaten
  • 1/2 cup Frozen mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, corn)
  • 1 tbsp Soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp Oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp Sesame oil
  • 1/4 tsp Ground white pepper
  • 2 tbsp Green onions, chopped
  • 1 tbsp Fried garlic (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Steam the siomai according to package instructions (about 10 minutes). Let cool slightly, then chop into small pieces.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add beaten eggs and scramble until just set. Remove from wok and set aside.
  3. Add remaining oil to the wok. Saut√© garlic until fragrant and lightly golden.
  4. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until thawed and heated through.
  5. Increase heat to high and add the cooked rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the chopped siomai and scrambled eggs back to the wok, mixing well to combine.
  7. Season with soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper. Continue stir-frying for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well incorporated and heated through.
  8. Turn off heat and mix in chopped green onions.
  9. Transfer to serving plates and garnish with fried garlic.
  10. Serve hot with chili oil or soy-vinegar dipping sauce on the side.

Chef's Notes

Variations: Add diced bell peppers or mushrooms for extra vegetables. For spice lovers, include chopped chili peppers or drizzle with sriracha.

Storage: Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days. Reheat in a pan with a little oil to restore texture.

Serving Suggestion: Pair with a simple cucumber salad or atchara (pickled papaya) for contrast.
